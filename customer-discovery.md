How many reviews did Pink's receive per week on average?
Estimated average: 65–75 reviews per week across all locations (20 locations)
What percentage did you actually respond to?
At Pink's, we respond to all reviews that include written feedback, regardless of the star rating.
How long did a single response take you — realistically?
On average, a single response takes about 10–15 seconds for straightforward 4–5 star reviews without negative feedback. For more complex cases—such as 1–3 star reviews or 4-star reviews with critical comments, the response time increases to roughly 30 seconds to 1 minute or more, depending on the issue raised.
What made a review hard to respond to vs. easy?
The most challenging reviews to respond to were negative ones. At Pink's, the goal was never to make the customer feel wrong, even when their perspective might not fully reflect the situation.This required setting aside any instinct to correct or defend, and instead focusing on resolving the issue and maintaining a positive long-term relationship. The difficulty came from balancing internal context with a public-facing response that prioritizes empathy, accountability, and customer satisfaction.
Did you ever copy-paste a response and regret it?
For higher-rated reviews, reusing responses wasn't a major issue since they typically focused on expressing gratitude and appreciation, which naturally leads to some overlap. At Pink's, we also maintained templates for common complaints. While these helped with efficiency, they introduced some risk: particularly with more complex or sensitive reviews. There were instances where a response was pasted incorrectly, details weren't properly customized, or a name wasn't updated. Additionally, when multiple customers shared the same experience (e.g., friends visiting together), they could receive very similar responses, which occasionally reduced the sense of personalization.
Would your family have paid $29/mo for a tool that handled this automatically?
Yes, we would. In fact, we already pay for similar tools. Currently, we spend between $30–$50 per month on Zapier to partially handle this process. However, the results are only moderate. It still requires significant manual input and editing, as the AI-generated responses are not consistently high quality, even when provided with detailed context.
What would have made you distrust a tool like this?
 A lack of response quality or consistency would be the main factor. If the tool generated replies that felt generic, inaccurate, or misaligned with our tone, it would quickly reduce trust. Additionally, any risk of errors, such as incorrect details, wrong names, or responses that don't properly address the customer's concern, would be a major concern, especially in public-facing interactions. Reliability and the ability to maintain brand voice are critical.
